Privacy Policy

Last Updated: June 17, 2026

StudyTrack ("we," "our," or "us") is committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, and disclose information when you use our mobile application, StudyTrack (the "App"). StudyTrack contains no ads, requires no account, and never sells your data.

1. Information We Collect

a. Personal Data

The App is designed to function without requiring you to create an account. We do not collect personal information such as your name, email address, or phone number.

b. Study Data (Local)

Your study sessions, focus time, subjects, and achievements are stored locally on your device using an internal database. This data stays on your device and is not transmitted to our servers.

c. Anonymous Usage Analytics

To understand how the App is used and to improve it, we collect anonymous, aggregated usage analytics using PostHog. This includes events such as when a study session is started or completed, which screens are viewed, and whether onboarding was finished. This data is tied only to a random, app-generated identifier.

We do not send your name, email, phone number, precise location, or any study content (such as subject names or notes) to our analytics provider. We never use this data to identify you, and we never sell it.

2. How We Use Information

We use anonymous analytics solely to maintain and improve the App's functionality, fix problems, and understand which features are helpful. Your study data is used only to power the App's features on your device.

3. Third-Party Services

The App relies on the following third-party services:

  • Google Play Services
  • PostHog (anonymous product analytics)

4. Your Choices

Because analytics is fully anonymous, it cannot be linked back to you. You can reset the random analytics identifier at any time by clearing the App's data, or by reinstalling the App.

5. Contact Us

If you have any questions or suggestions about our Privacy Policy, do not hesitate to contact us at ddtechstudio8@gmail.com.
